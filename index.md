## Hi, I'm Ashutosh

I'm a Computer Science undergraduate fascinated by robotics, specifically, how you get an autonomous system to actually understand and navigate the world around it.

My research interests live at the intersection of computer vision, robot learning, and visual navigation. I'm particularly drawn to making these systems work under real-world constraints, which, as an undergrad without access to a rack of LiDARs and stereo rigs, mostly means figuring out how much you can do with a single cheap camera.

Turns out, the answer is: quite a lot, if you're willing to read enough papers.

I enjoy working on problems end-to-end, from the core algorithm all the way to something running on actual hardware. Right now that means monocular vision for navigation, benchmarking vision-based algorithms, and poking at sensor distillation for autonomous driving.

The long-term goal is to push what intelligent robots can do in messy, unpredictable, real-world environments, ideally without needing a sensor suite that costs more than my college fees.

Read more about my [Projects](/projects/) and [Research](/research/). 

Outside of research, I watch too much television: Game of Thrones, The Big Bang Theory, and Modern Family are personal favourites. I also enjoy photography, which has given me a surprising appreciation for optics and lighting that occasionally comes in handy.
